Authors:

Jing-Xuan Zhang, Zhen-Hua Ling, Li-Rong Dai

Abstract:

In this paper, we present a method for non-parallel sequence-to-sequence voice conversion based on disentangled representations learning. We assume acoustic features can be factorized into linguistic and speaker related information. Voice conversion can be achieved by preserving the linguistic rep- resentation of source utterance while replacing the speaker representation with the target one. We built our model under the framework of encoder-decoder. Encoders are used to ex- tract hidden representations which the decoder consumes for recovering acoustic features. For learning disentangled linguistic representations, we proposed two strategies. First, additional text inputs are used to extract linguistic hidden representations, which are pulled together with those from audio inputs to share the same linguistic space. Second, we used an adversarial training method to further wipe out the speaker related descriptions from the linguistic embeddings from audio signals. Meanwhile, speaker representations are extracted by a speaker encoder. Our model is built with the sequence-to-sequence network therefore there is no the constrain of the frame-by-frame conversion. A multi- speaker pre-training and adaptation on specific conversion pair training strategy is used. Experiments were conducted to compare the proposed method with strong baselines. The results showed that our method obtained higher similarity and naturalness compared to the top rank method in Voice Conversion Challenge 2018 (VCC2018). And the performance of proposed method was closed to state-of-art parallel voice conversion method. Even with 5 times smaller training data of conversion pair, our model achieved comparable results to VCC2018 baseline. Ablation tests were further conducted to validate proposed strategies for disentangling and pre-training.
